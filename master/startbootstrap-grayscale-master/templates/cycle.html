It is all about how she is

The hormonal cycle

The female hormonal cycle has been a subject a lot of taboo and while it is not a controversial subject anymore, the research of its impact on training and sport is only slowly trickling through. Let's have a quick remainder of the biological processes and then dive in on the implications for movement.

The average cycle is 28 days but this can vary from women to women. The cycle starts on day 1 of period. At this point the oestrogen level is at its lowest and starts increasing as the days go. Oestrogen is responsible for the production of serotonin to the mood may be low during the early days. The first day of period is also the first day of the follicular phase during which the body prepares for ovulation (by releasing follicular stimulating hormone (FSH)). Oestrogen peaks at around day 10 and in the build up to this time many women feel confident and energetic. Around day 12 the oestrogen levels along lutenising hormone (LH) surges which causes ovulation and an egg is released from the fallopian tubes. Ovulation itself happens around day 15ish, after which all hell breaks loose; progesterone levels rise to tell uterus to start thickening and oestrogen levels dip (but it starts going up again as the body goes into a nesting state). Both oestrogen and progesterone reach peak levels at day 5 before menstruation. This is when PMS can be experienced. If fertilised egg is not available, the body sheds the lining out and goes back to day 1.
